The Fruit Seller's Fortune

A humble offering of love transforms into jewels by the Lord's grace

The Story

One morning in Vrindavan, an old fruit seller woman walked through the lanes calling out her wares. Hearing her voice, little Krishna came running from inside the house, eager to get fruits. In His excitement and haste, He grabbed some grains from a container to offer in exchange — but His tiny hands could barely hold them, and most of the grains slipped through His fingers and fell to the ground before He could reach her.

The fruit seller looked at the few remaining grains in the child's small palm barely anything at all. But she was moved by the innocence and sweetness of the little boy before her. Without a second thought, she filled His hands with as many fruits as He could carry, asking for nothing in return. Her heart was simply full of love.

That pure and selfless offering made without expectation, without calculation, with only love — did not go unnoticed by the Lord. As the fruit seller turned to leave, she glanced down at her basket and stopped in wonder. Every single fruit remaining in her basket had transformed into brilliant jewels and precious gems, shining and glittering in the morning light.

This is the eternal teaching of the fruit seller's lila that the Lord is not purchased by wealth, learning, or grand offerings. He is won only by love offered without condition. The fruit seller gave a handful of fruit to a child and received in return the greatest treasure in all the worlds the grace of the Supreme Lord Himself.

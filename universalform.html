Brahmananda Darshana

Mother Yashoda beholds the entire universe within Krishna's mouth

The Story

One day the young cowherd boys came running to Mother Yashoda with a complaint Krishna had been eating mud. When she asked Krishna directly, He looked up at her with wide innocent eyes and denied it completely. "Mother, I have not eaten mud. All my friends are lying. If you don't believe me, you can look inside my mouth yourself."

Mother Yashoda, half-amused and half-concerned, asked Krishna to open His mouth. And Krishna the Supreme Lord, who is the cause of all causes opened His mouth, revealing what no mother could ever be prepared to see. Inside that small mouth of a child was the entire universe.

There was the sky, the stars, the planets, the sun and the moon. There were mountains, oceans, forests, and rivers. There was Vrindavan itself with the Yamuna river flowing through it, the cows grazing, the gopas playing, and Yashoda herself standing there looking into Krishna's mouth. The past, the present, and the future. All of creation, complete and infinite, contained within the mouth of her little son.

For a moment, Yashoda was overwhelmed she began to understand that this was no ordinary child. But then Krishna, out of His infinite compassion and maternal love for her, withdrew His yogamaya potency. In an instant, she forgot everything she had seen. She simply closed her son's mouth, held Him close, and her heart overflowed with love, the pure, simple love of a mother for her child. The Lord had allowed her to see the truth, and then lovingly restored her motherly blindness, for that is the sweetness of His lila.

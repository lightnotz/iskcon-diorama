Kaliya Daman

Krishna subdues the serpent Kaliya

📸 Diorama Photo Coming Soon

The Story

Long ago, in the sacred waters of the Yamuna river near Vrindavan, there lived a fearsome multi-headed serpent named Kaliya. His poisonous venom had made the waters deadly, killing anyone who drank from the river or even came near it.

One day, young Krishna and his cowherd friends were playing on the banks of the Yamuna. Their ball fell into the river, and Krishna fearlessly jumped in to retrieve it. Kaliya rose up in fury, wrapping his massive coils around Krishna.

But Krishna expanded his body and broke free from the serpent's grip. He then leaped onto Kaliya's many heads and began to dance — a divine dance that subdued the serpent completely. Kaliya's wives begged Krishna for mercy, and the compassionate Lord spared Kaliya's life, ordering him to leave the Yamuna and never return.

From that day, the Yamuna's waters became pure and sacred once more, and the people of Vrindavan were free from fear.
